The 20-minute "never zero" flow +

On a day everything's gone sideways and you're on a set, in London, or just buried, this is the get-out-of-jail card. No kit, 20 minutes, full body. Pick it in any day's dropdown and it ticks legs, upper and core in one go, so a chaotic week still closes covered.

Warm up · 2 min

  • Arm circles, leg swings, slow cat-cow, gentle hip circles. Loosen, don't strain.

Main · 3 rounds, 30–45s rest between rounds

  • Bodyweight squats × 12
  • Press-ups × 8 (drop to knees, no shame)
  • Reverse lunges × 8 each side
  • Plank × 30 seconds
  • Glute bridges × 15
  • Dead bug × 8 each side

Cool down · 2–3 min

  • Downward dog, cobra, child's pose, seated forward fold, a few slow breaths.
The posture and low-belly bit: the plank and dead bug are doing that job. They train the deep core that holds your midsection tight and your posture upright. Move steady, breathe, don't rush them.
The Wednesday fix: run + legs +

Chidge runs on Wednesdays in Stratford, so you run. But legs need strength work too and there's nowhere clean to put a separate session. The fix: bolt a quick legs circuit onto the end of the run while everything's warm.

The run

  • 3–5k with Chidge, whatever pace suits the day.

Legs finisher · 2 rounds, 12–15 min

  • Goblet squats × 12
  • Romanian deadlifts (bodyweight) × 10
  • Walking lunges × 10 each side
  • Calf raises × 15
  • Wall sit × 30 seconds
Why this works: your muscles are already warm from the run, so the legs circuit slots in naturally. Even one round ticks the "legs" box for the week. Two rounds is the full session.
The rules, baked in +
  • Most runs easy and conversational, the pace where you could chat. Only about one run in five should be hard.
  • Never two hard sessions back to back. This planner flags it if you try.
  • Legs never land on the same day as a hard run. One job per day here, so you can't accidentally double up.
  • Long run grows by no more than 10% a week. 7k to 7.5 to 8, not 7 straight to 10.
  • Cycling is your flex lever, not a daily must. Use it when your legs are sore or you want cardio without the pounding.
  • Rest is a session, not a gap. One or two full days a week. This is where you actually get fitter.
  • Never zero. On a wrecked day, the 20-minute fallback keeps the week alive.
